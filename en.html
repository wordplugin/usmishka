Bringing smiles to children in Ukraine

"Usmishka" means "smile" in Ukrainian. Led by Hideto "Pika" Chujo on the ground in Ukraine, we support children and women affected by the war and promote exchange between Japan and Ukraine.

Our Activities

What we do

SUPPORT FOR CHILDRENSupporting children

We support children traumatized by the war and children suffering from serious illness — visiting pediatric hospitals, holding events at evacuation shelters, and delivering gifts to bring back their smiles.

Read more (JP)

HUMANITARIAN AIDHumanitarian aid

We deliver relief supplies and help secure housing. When the Kakhovka dam collapsed, we distributed generators and power banks from day one. We also co-operate a shelter prioritizing people with disabilities and run a dental-care "Health Project".

Read more (JP)

CULTURAL EXCHANGECultural exchange

We share Japanese culture — sumo, kendama, origami, and music — with the people of Ukraine, including inviting young Ukrainian sumo wrestlers to Japan's national Wanpaku Sumo tournament.

Read more (JP)

MATCHINGMatching

We strengthen people-to-people ties between Japan and Ukraine and contribute to Ukraine's recovery, operating the "Usmishka" Japan Center at Dnipro National University and building partnerships between universities, municipalities, and organizations.

Read more (JP)

Wanpaku Sumo Ukraine

Turning anxiety into hope

Courage, courtesy, gratitude

CourageCourtesyGratitude

Since 2023 we have held the Wanpaku Sumo Ukraine tournament every year. Even interrupted by air-raid sirens, the children give their all in the ring — and the winners are invited to the national Wanpaku Sumo tournament in Japan.

"I want to win on the dream stage in Japan and dedicate the victory to my father serving in the army." Your support makes dreams like this come true. The tournaments have been widely covered by Ukrainian media including public broadcaster Suspilne and ICTV.

History

Our journey
May 2022

Into wartime Ukraine

Hideto Chujo entered Ukraine alone, beginning support caravans to evacuation shelters in Lviv, Bucha, Kyiv, Dnipro, Uzhhorod and beyond. His yellow Pikachu hoodie brought smiles back to children's faces.

July 2022

Usmishka Association founded

Established to sustain support from Japan. Published the on-the-ground report "Walking Through Wartime Ukraine", featured in the Asahi Shimbun and other media.

Oct–Dec 2022

Support Caravan #1

Using your donations, delivered winter goods, portable lights and power, solar panels and toys by truck across the country. Covered by NHK and local press; full accounts published.

Dec 2022 – Jan 2023

Pika-Santa Caravan (#2)

Ukraine celebrates Christmas twice — Gregorian and Julian. Visited shelters, hospitals and families of children with disabilities in Kherson, Mykolaiv, Zaporizhzhia, Dnipro and Kharkiv as Santa, with events at Kyiv's largest pediatric hospital and an art museum in Dnipro.

Jan–Feb 2023

Broadcast nationwide in Ukraine

Usmishka's work was aired across Ukraine by the national broadcaster and reported in Poland. Began building "Usmishka House", a learning and play space at a shelter in Dnipro.

Mar 2023

First Wanpaku Sumo Ukraine tournament

Co-hosted a children's sumo tournament with Ukraine's Ministry of Sport — the first of its kind, covered by Ukrainian news.

June 2023

Kakhovka dam collapse — emergency response

Distributed portable generators in Kherson from day one, plus 100 power banks and 10 power stations purchased with donations, working alongside the deputy mayor. Received official invitations and letters of gratitude from the city and oblast; covered by US press.

July 2023

First young wrestlers travel to Japan

Tournament champions Mykola (Mykolaiv) and Makar (Rivne) were invited to the national Wanpaku Sumo tournament at Ryogoku Kokugikan in Tokyo on July 30.

Nov 2023

Departing for a stay of over six months

Left Haneda for Ukraine again, continuing Caravan #3 near the front lines — with every yen publicly accounted for.

Mar 2024

2nd Wanpaku Sumo Ukraine tournament

Held March 8 at the Olympic Reserve Stadium in Dnipro — from building the ring to the awards ceremony — and covered nationwide by Suspilne, ICTV and live TV.

May 2024

Kharkiv flood relief

Responded to requests from Kharkiv authorities and fellow aid workers after dam flooding; also helped repair damaged homes near the Dnipro hydroelectric plant.

Jul–Aug 2024

Three young wrestlers in Japan; hospital support

Egor (Mykolaiv) and two others competed at Ryogoku Kokugikan. Emergency donations supported Kyiv's Okhmatdyt children's hospital after it was struck in July.

2025

Health Project

Ran the Usmishka Health Project with dentists — cavity treatment, oral care and brushing lessons for children unable to evacuate far. Supported pianist Tempei Nakamura's Kyiv concert in October.

Oct 2025

Ukrainian-Japanese Center opens in Dnipro

After signing an agreement with Oles Honchar Dnipro National University in August, the Ukrainian-Japanese Center opened on campus on October 27. Working with the Ukraine-Japan Center in Kyiv, it offers Japanese language classes, calligraphy lessons and other cultural programs. Read the university's article.

Dec 2025

3rd tournament champions head to the Hakuho Cup

Winners of the 3rd Ukraine Children's Sumo Usmishka National Tournament were selected for Japan's Hakuho Cup; Makar returned to Japan for a second time.

May 2026

4th sumo tournament

Working with the Ukrainian Sumo Federation, completed a national tournament for children and students despite air-raid interruptions. Regular visits to Okhmatdyt children's hospital continue, including making good-luck badges with children before surgery.

2026

Over 100 caravans — Caravan #5 ongoing

Fighting still continues in the east and south. Supply runs, hospital visits, cultural exchange — under one motto: "May every child live a happy childhood."

Please support our work

Donation

In today's Ukraine, supplies exist "where they exist" — what relief really needs is information and money. With your donations we purchase supplies locally, and Hideto Chujo delivers them directly to children in Ukraine.

To show that every yen reaches the children, we publish a full breakdown of how donations are spent. Donations start from 3,000 JPY.

Pika Chujo's Usmishka Caravan #5

Bank transfer (10,000+ JPY)

By registered mail: Usmishka Association, 2246 Mure, Mure-cho, Takamatsu, Kagawa 761-0196, Japan.
Donations are made to a non-profit general incorporated association and handled under our donation rules.

How donations are used

  • Gifts for children (toothbrushes, chopsticks, toys, plush animals, etc.)
  • Power sources such as solar panels and batteries
  • Event supplies and event costs
  • Food and drink
  • Medicine, medical equipment and medical support (Health Project)
  • Transport, travel and fuel costs

Message from the Chairman

We want to provide support from the ground-level perspective that governments and international organizations find difficult. Saving children deprived of a safe living environment by war and violence requires not only systems and care, but face-to-face relationships. Cultural exchange also encourages children emotionally and helps their potential grow. We support Hideto Chujo's work on the ground and use ICT to deliver support from Japan to the children of Ukraine. Thank you for your understanding and cooperation. — Nagaaki Kamada, Chairman
